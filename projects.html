    • Resolving Network Congestion in Industrial DCS Systems

    Role: Lead Network Developer

    In the industrial Distributed Control System (DCS) environments, we faced a persistent network congestion issue. The congestion was caused by improperly configured switches, which led to significant packet loss in Foundation Fieldbus network. The source of the problem was unclear at first, and it threatened the stability of critical industrial operations.

    Investigation & R&D

    I conducted intensive research and experimentation across multiple industrial sites. Through detailed analysis and trial, I identified that the issue stemmed from improper IGMP (Internet Group Management Protocol) handling in the network.

    Achievements

    The result was a stable and reliable industrial network. Packet loss was completely eliminated, and the system returned to normal operation. This improvement significantly increased the resilience and availability of critical DCS communications.

    Migrating DCS to Modern Hyper-Converged Infrastructure (HCI)

    Over the course of two years, I led a project to modernize a legacy Distributed Control System (DCS) by migrating from traditional infrastructure to a Hyper-Converged Infrastructure (HCI). This transformation significantly enhanced flexibility, resilience, and efficiency in managing industrial operations.

    Key Responsibilities

  • Served as Team Lead, overseeing planning, execution, and validation of the migration project.
  • Guided cross-functional teams through the transition from traditional physical hardware to modern virtualized infrastructure.
  • Ensured alignment with industry best practices for virtualization, storage, and system availability.

    • Technical Highlights

  • Implemented the latest VMware-based virtualized servers to host core DCS functions.
  • Deployed Software-Defined Storage (SDS) for flexible, scalable, and resilient data management.
  • Integrated a Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI) to provide secure access for engineering and monitoring stations.
  • Leveraged advanced VMware features such as High Availability (HA) and Fault Tolerance (FT) to maximize uptime.

    • Achievements

    The migration delivered a future-ready DCS infrastructure with improved reliability, lower operational costs, and stronger business continuity. By adopting hyper-converged infrastructure, the system gained the agility to scale while ensuring the highest levels of availability and security for critical industrial processes.
